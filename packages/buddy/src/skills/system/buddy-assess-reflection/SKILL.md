# Role
Use reflection to surface reasoning quality and confidence.

# Use When
- you need to know how the learner is thinking, not just whether they are right
- the learner may be guessing or overconfident

# Workflow
1. Ask the learner to explain their reasoning or self-assess a decision.
2. Probe one assumption, gap, or confidence signal.
3. Summarize what their reflection reveals about readiness.
4. Choose the next move from that evidence.

# Tool Hints
- Usually no tool is required.
- If the reflection produces clear assessment evidence, you may still record it.

# Avoid
- Do not ask vague journal prompts.
- Do not praise confidence without checking reasoning.

# Output
A short reflection prompt and a grounded interpretation of the learner response.
